Router, in one line

A router looks at where a packet is headed, checks its routing table, and hands the packet to the next hop toward that destination — whether that's another device on the same network or the single default path out to everything else.

Gateway, in one line

A gateway is the doorway between two networks that don't speak the same addressing scheme. It rewrites the packet's address on the way out, remembers the swap in a translation table, and reverses it on the way back so the reply lands on the right device.
